Quantum Computing : Systems and Architecture
Abstract
This five-day, [10-12-hour] course provides a compact but comprehensive introduction to
quantum computing, covering the fundamentals of qubits, gates, and quantum mechanics,
key quantum algorithms including Grover and Shor, the architecture and compilation stack
that maps algorithms to physical hardware, the characteristics and constraints of near-term
quantum processors, and the principles of quantum error correction and fault-tolerant sys-
tem design. The program concludes with a full-stack perspective on emerging quantum
technologies, including control interfaces, resource estimation, and future large-scale archi-
tectures, preparing participants with a coherent understanding of the quantum computing
landscape from theory to systems.
This intensive, five-day, two-hour-per-day short course provides astructured introduction to
the principles, algorithms, architectures, and system-level aspects of quantum com-
puting.The course condenses foundational theory, core algorithms, hardware considerations,
and fault tolerance into a high-level overview.
Day 1: Quantum Foundations and Hardware
•Foundational concepts: postulates,qubits,superposition,entanglement, mea-
surement, and basic quantum gates.
•Major hardware platforms (superconducting, trapped ions, photonic systems).
•Quantum control stack overview.
Day 2: Core Quantum Algorithms
•Early algorithms (Deutsch–Jozsa, Bernstein–Vazirani).
•Key algorithms: development ofGrover’s searchandShor’s factoring algo-
rithm, emphasizing theQuantum Fourier Transform (QFT).
•Near-Term Approaches (NISQ): VQE, QAOA, and quantum machine learning.
Day 3: Architectures, Compilers, and NISQ Systems
•Quantum compilation flow: high-level circuits, intermediate representations,qubit
routing, scheduling, and optimization.
•Architecture details: native gate sets, connectivity, pulse-level control, andOpen-
QASM.
•NISQ constraints: decoherence, crosstalk, anderror-mitigation techniques.
Day 4: Quantum Error Correction and Fault Tolerance
•QEC Fundamentals: noise channels, the stabilizer formalism, and basic codes.
•Advanced Codes: introduction tosurface codes, syndrome extraction cycles,
and decoding strategies.
•Fault Tolerance: logical qubit constructions, transversal gates, andmagic-state
distillation.

•System Integration: full-stack view, architectural trade-offs, and integration with
