*Title:* Computational studies of universal droplet pinch-off dynamics and
dripping-to-jetting transition
*Date:* January  23rd, 2026
*Time:* 2:30 PM
*Venue:* A1, 102
*Join with Google Meet:* meet.google.com/eyi-xeve-jmy
*Abstract:*  This APS report presents the validation studies conducted for
the developed solver. The test cases, such as Rayleigh-Taylor instability,
drop under oscillation, and drop under shear, are designed to validate the
capability of the developed 2-dimensional phase-field lattice Boltzmann
solver for two-phase flows. Further, the two-dimensional solver is extended
to three dimensions in space, and test cases, such as single-bubble rise in
a quiescent, denser fluid column, along with drop oscillation and drop
under shear flow, are performed. After performing the validation studies, a
3D drop pinch-off problem is solved using the current developed algorithm.
The solver captures all physical phenomena, including drop growth, necking,
and the formation of primary and satellite drops. The necking follows the
scaling laws reported in the literature, and the transition from dripping
to jetting is captured at the critical Weber number, as predicted.
