*Mr. Pavan Bavanavar (Roll no. cy24dp008)*, a PhD scholar in the Department

*Title: *Synthesis of Nickel Nanocluster and its Application in Urea
Synthesis by Electrocoupling of Nitrate and Carbon Dioxide.
*Venue:* A2-119
*Date:* January 27th, 2026
*Time:* 2:00 - 3:00 PM
All are welcome to attend.

*Abstract:* Metal nanoclusters (NCs) are widely recognized as a critical
bridge between molecular metal complexes and metal nanoparticles (NPs).
Compared to conventional catalysts based on metal NPs, metal NCs have
well-defined structures in both the arrangement of metal atoms in the core
as well as the ligand-binding modes on the surface; they possess unique
size-dependent catalytic, optical, and electronic properties. In this
regard, several thiolate-protected gold (Au) and silver (Ag) NCs have been
reported as model catalysts due to their ease of synthesis, well-resolved
crystal structure and high stability. However, as an alternative to
noble-metal-based nanocatalysts, nickel (Ni) nanoclusters have emerged as
an important catalytic material owing to their earth-abundance, rich redox
chemistry, and strong metal–substrate interactions. Herein, we have shown
the synthesis of nickel clusters, Ni6OL12 and Ni6CL12, stabilised by new
thiolate ligands and their application in electrochemical synthesis of urea
using CO2 and NO3- as substrates. Electrochemical studies revealed
catalytic activity for CO₂ and NO₃⁻ reduction, and product analysis after
controlled-potential electrolysis (CPE) at various potentials confirmed the
formation of urea and NH₃. Furthermore, the role of the ligand environment
was elucidated through comparative studies with an analogous nickel
cluster, which features a single-atom ligand modification, highlighting the
crucial influence of the ligand on catalytic efficiency and selectivity.
