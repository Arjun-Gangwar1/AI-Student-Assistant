*Title*: A Hybrid Islanding Detection Strategy for Synchronverter-Based
Grid-Forming Inverters Under LVRT Constraints in Inverter-Dominated Power
Systems.

*Abstract*: The growing deployment of grid-forming inverter-based resources
(IBRs), particularly Synchronverter-based grid-forming inverters (GFMs),
has introduced new challenges for islanding detection in inverter-dominated
power systems. Reliable and grid-code-compliant detection has therefore
become increasingly important. Conventional methods demonstrate limited
effectiveness in multi-inverter environments and often result in large
non-detection zones (NDZs). These challenges are further intensified by
low-voltage ride-through (LVRT) requirements. This work investigates a
hybrid islanding detection strategy for Synchronverter-based GFMs, which
coordinates LVRT behavior with complementary detection features. The
proposed approach is currently being evaluated using a modified CIGRE
low-voltage benchmark system with mixed grid-forming and grid-following
inverters. Initial results suggest promising potential for future
low-inertia power systems.
